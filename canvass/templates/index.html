Canvass

Canvass is an initiative driven by Fedora to collect statistics on Linux usage and hardware specifications of users. Canvass is used to further Linux development and gain an greater understanding of the userbase.

How it works

Canvass is an opt-in program during the Fedora installation process. If a user decides to opt-in, their hardware information, their country, and their Linux specifications will be sent to the central Canvass server. These records are then used to generate objective statistics.

More about Canvas

Cavass is open source. It's source can be found on GitHub. You can hack on it, or run your own instance. Canvass is the successor to many prior projects with similar goals.
